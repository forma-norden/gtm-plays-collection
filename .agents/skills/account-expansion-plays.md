# account-expansion-plays

Use this skill to design plays aimed at existing customers. Growth from within
the base is cheaper and closes faster than net-new acquisition.

## 1. The "Champion Left" Play
**Trigger:** Your core advocate at a customer account leaves the company.
**Risk:** Huge churn risk. The new person will bring in their own tools.
**Timing:** Immediate (as soon as the departure is known).
**Action:** 
1. The CSM immediately identifies the interim owner and secures a meeting to "handoff" knowledge and preserve continuity.
2. Provide a 90-day ROI report highlighting what the tool has achieved so the new owner views it as a win, not a cost.

## 2. The "Cross-Sell Trigger" Play
**Trigger:** A customer successfully hits a specific usage milestone in Product A, making them eligible for Product B.
**Timing:** 7-14 days post-milestone.
**Action:** 
"Noticed your team just crossed [Milestone] using [Product A]. Typically, teams hitting that volume start running into [New Problem]. Usually, they bolt on [Product B] to handle it. Want me to flip on a 30-day trial of B for your team?"

## 3. The "Seat Expansion" Play
**Trigger:** 90%+ utilization of current seat licenses, or new hires joining the specific department using the tool.
**Timing:** 30 days before contract renewal, OR immediately upon seeing new hires on LinkedIn/Clearbit.
**Action:**
"Saw you guys just brought on 5 new SDRs (congrats!). Looks like you only have 1 open license left. I can co-term 4 new licenses to your current contract so they have access on day 1. Want me to send that over?"

## 4. The "Multi-Threading the Base" Play
**Trigger:** You only have one single point of contact (single-threaded) at an Enterprise account.
**Timing:** 6 months before renewal.
**Action:**
The AE/CSM proactively reaches out to the champion's boss: "Your team has been crushing it with [Tool]. We just generated $X in value over the last 6 months globally. Would love 15 minutes to walk you through the Q3 roadmap and how we map to your broader Q3 goals."

## Rules of Expansion

1. **CSM/AE Alignment:** Never run an outbound expansion play without the explicit green light from the active Customer Success Manager.
2. **Value First:** You cannot ask for more money if they aren't fully utilizing what they already bought.
3. **The Executive Review:** Treat expansions like net-new deals. Build an ROI deck. Don't assume the renewal is automatic.

## Output Contract
When designing an expansion play, provide:
- The strict criteria for account eligibility (e.g., must have health score > 80).
- The internal handoff process between CS and Sales.
- The exact messaging cadence focused on ROI and risk mitigation.
