# outbound-sequence-plays

Use this skill to design structural outbound sequences for cold pipelines
when no direct buying signals are present (Tier 3 plays).

## The Standard Agitation Sequence
**Target:** Broad ICP list, highly specific persona.
**Duration:** 14-21 days.
**Goal:** Generate a reply by agitating a known common pain point.

### Step 1: The Hypothesis (Day 1)
- **Channel:** Email.
- **Premise:** "Since you run X at Y, assuming you struggle with Z. We solve Z."
- **Focus:** Sharp pain statement + mechanism of solution + soft CTA.

### Step 2: The Bump (Day 3)
- **Channel:** Email.
- **Premise:** "Thoughts on the above?"
- **Focus:** Keep it under 10 words. High reply rate generator.

### Step 3: The Proof (Day 6)
- **Channel:** Email.
- **Premise:** "Forgot to mention, here's exactly how we fixed this for [Competitor/Peer]..."
- **Focus:** Case study, hard metric, screenshot of dashboard. Provide tangible evidence.

### Step 4: The Pivot (Day 10)
- **Channel:** Email.
- **Premise:** "If Z isn't a priority, maybe A is?"
- **Focus:** Shift to the secondary value prop or a completely different use case for your platform.

### Step 5: The Breakup (Day 14-17)
- **Channel:** Email.
- **Premise:** "Not the right time. Feel free to access this free resource."
- **Focus:** Zero pressure. Provide an ungated link to a high-value piece of content. Leave a positive final impression.

## The Account-Based Multi-Threading Play
**Target:** Enterprise accounts (Tier 1/2). Multiple stakeholders.
**Duration:** 30+ Days.
**Goal:** Map the buying committee and find the champion.

- **Phase 1 (Below the Line):** Sequence Managers/ICs. Focus entirely on their daily workflows and tactical pain points. Goal is to get them to validate the pain.
- **Phase 2 (Above the Line):** Sequence VPs/CXOs. If a Manager confirms pain but won't take a meeting, email the VP: "Director X mentioned your team is fighting [Pain]. Typically, addressing that unlocks $Y..."
- **Phase 3 (Cross-Functional):** Sequence IT/Ops/Finance. Focus on compliance, integration, or cost savings rather than feature sets.

## Sequencing Rules

1. **Volume over Length:** 5 short emails perform better than 2 long ones.
2. **One idea per email:** Never pitch 3 features in one message.
3. **The 3-Day Rule:** Wait at least 72 hours between touches to avoid spam filters.
4. **Channel Switching:** If calling, call *after* the email opens, not before.

## Output Contract
When designing a sequence play, provide:
- The total duration and number of steps.
- The channel mix (Email/Call/LI).
- The specific angle/premise for each individual step.
- The overarching goal (Meeting vs Mapping).
